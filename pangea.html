Difference Between Modern Earth and Pangea

Modern Earth is very different from how the planet looked millions of years ago. Today, Earth is divided into multiple continents such as Africa, Asia, Europe, North America, South America, Australia, and Antarctica. These continents are separated by vast oceans including the Atlantic, Pacific, Indian, Arctic, and Southern oceans.

In contrast, during the time of Pangea, all continents were united into a single massive landmass. There were no separate continents as we know them today. Instead, Earth had only one supercontinent surrounded by a single enormous ocean known as the Tethys Ocean.

Because everything was connected in Pangea, climates, ecosystems, and animal migration were very different. Species could travel across the land without needing to cross oceans, which explains why similar fossils have been found on continents that are now far apart.

Over millions of years, tectonic plate movement caused Pangea to slowly break apart. This process formed the continents and oceans we see today, shaping Earth's modern geography and influencing climate, biodiversity, and human civilization.
