Mesoamerican Civilizations

Olmecs

The Olmecs are considered the first great Mesoamerican civilization. They lived along the Gulf Coast of present-day Mexico, mainly in the regions of Veracruz and Tabasco. Their civilization developed around 1200 BCE and is famous for the colossal stone heads they carved.

The Olmecs had advanced knowledge of agriculture, growing crops such as maize, beans, and squash. They also developed early forms of writing, religious rituals, and complex social structures that influenced later civilizations.

Mayans

The Maya civilization lived in southern Mexico, Guatemala, Belize, Honduras, and El Salvador. They are known for their impressive cities, pyramids, and temples built in the rainforest.

The Maya made major advances in mathematics, astronomy, and writing. They created a complex calendar system and used a base-20 number system, including the concept of zero. Their society was deeply connected to religion and the observation of the stars.
